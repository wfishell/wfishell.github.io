Research Interests

I leverage neuromorphic computing and formal methods to drive advances in deep learning systems. I am interested in different modes of learning. Specifically, I am fascinated by how biological and logic based modes of learning and how these can be used to create improved machine learning architectures

Synthesizing State Space Models: Learning Meets Logic

Currently under submission for the FMCAD Student Forum, I am investigating how specification mining from finite traces can learn to synthesize reactive systems, compared to training of SSMs. We are currently researching how well both methods learn various reactive systems with the goal of using automata learning and specification mining to generate initializations for State Space Models for faster convergence

FMCAD Paper

SNNs Are Not Transformers Yet: The Architectural Problems for SNNs in Modeling Long-Range Dependencies

Spiking Neural Networks are a biologically plausible architectures with much greater energy efficiency then current deep learning architectures. Despite this, these models have faced slow adoption. We derive a sample complexity for these models and show how this scales with respect to longer input sequences. This demonstrates some of the weakness in these models in being adopted for language modeling. This work was presented at the SHDA Workshop in conjunction with the International Conference on Supercomputing
